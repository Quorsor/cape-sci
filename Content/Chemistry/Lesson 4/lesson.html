MOLECULAR SHAPES: VSEPR

 

Using VSEPR

Before applying VSEPR to a molecule or complex ion it is necessary to know the connectivity, that is, which atoms are bonded together. With a species of formula AXn this often gives no problem, especially if X is a monovalent atom or group.

 

Sometimes it is not so obvious, and a useful rule (which does not apply to hydrides or organic groups) is that the central atom is usually the least electronegative one. 

 

The valence shell electron pair repulsion (VSEPR) model is based on the idea that both bonding and nonbonding electron pairs in the valence shell of an atom ‘repel’ each other. This idea is useful but can be misleading if taken too literally. Detailed calculations show that the shape of a molecule is determined by a combination of factors, of which the electrostatic repulsion between electrons is not the most important.
